# How Difficult is the Roopkund Trek?

The Roopkund Trek is considered **moderate to difficult**. Here’s what you need to know before you go:

## Difficulty Breakdown
- **Altitude:** Max altitude ~15,700 ft (4,780 m). Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS) risk above 10,000 ft.
- **Terrain:** Steep ascents, rocky sections, snowfields (May/Oct), long walking days (6–8 hrs).
- **Weather:** Rapid changes, cold nights, snow possible in spring/autumn.

## Who Should (and Shouldn’t) Go?
- **Recommended for:**
  - Fit trekkers with prior high-altitude experience
  - Beginners with strong fitness and prep
- **Not recommended for:**
  - Those with heart/lung conditions
  - Children under 12
  - Anyone unable to walk 10–12 km/day

## Altitude Impact
- **Acclimatization:** Spend 1–2 days at base (Lohajung) before trek.
- **Hydration:** Drink plenty of water, avoid alcohol.
- **Symptoms:** Headache, nausea, fatigue—descend if severe.
