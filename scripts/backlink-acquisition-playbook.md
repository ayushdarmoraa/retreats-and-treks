## 3 GUEST POST PITCHES (Ready to Send)

### Pitch 1: "How to Build a Trekking Progression in the Indian Himalayas"
**Target:** General outdoor/adventure blogs
**Angle:** Most people attempt hard treks too early. This piece maps a structured 4-trek progression from 3,850m to 4,800m in a single Himalayan region — Garhwal — showing how each trek builds skills for the next.
**Link opportunity:** Natural link to /treks/garhwal-himalayas as "the complete route overview"
**Word count:** 1,200–1,500 words
**Unique value:** Original progression framework (Brahmatal → Kuari → Roopkund → Pangarchulla) that no other site maps explicitly

### Pitch 2: "The Moderate vs Challenging Decision: When Are You Ready for a Harder Trek?"
**Target:** Trekking-specific blogs, fitness blogs
**Angle:** Trekkers consistently overestimate their readiness for challenging routes. This piece provides concrete fitness benchmarks and decision criteria — when to step up from moderate (Brahmatal/Kuari Pass) to challenging (Roopkund/Pangarchulla), based on altitude history, fitness markers, and psychological readiness.
**Link opportunity:** Natural link to /treks/brahmatal-vs-kuari-pass or /treks/roopkund-vs-pangarchulla as "detailed comparison"
**Word count:** 1,000–1,200 words
**Unique value:** Specific, testable fitness benchmarks (jog distance, stair climb capacity, loaded walk metrics) — not generic "be fit" advice

### Pitch 3: "What I Learned Training for a 4,800m Trek in a Flat City"
**Target:** Travel blogs, lifestyle/wellness blogs, fitness blogs
**Angle:** First-person-style framework piece. How to prepare for extreme altitude when you live at sea level — the specific adaptations, training hacks (stairmaster as altitude simulator), and what actually matters vs what doesn't. Positioned as a "city dweller's guide to Himalayan fitness."
**Link opportunity:** Natural link to /treks/garhwal-himalayas/fitness-guide as "the full 8-week plan"
**Word count:** 800–1,200 words
**Unique value:** The fitness guide page is yours — no other trekking site has a structured 8-week plan with benchmarks by trek difficulty
